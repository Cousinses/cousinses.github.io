Kizomba

Kizomba is an African dance characterized by close partner connection and smooth, grounded movement. In kizomba, there is no “basic step”. Instead, movement with the music and connection with the partner is the priority and fancier moves are more often added in European kizomba and Urban Kiz.

Kizomba naturally evolved from Angolan semba and has since become increasingly popular - which has lead to it evolving even further into styles such as Urban Kiz (characterized by more linear movement, more frequent syncopation and breaks, and an upright stance.)

Bachata

Bachata is a style of dance that originated in the Dominican Republic. It is danced widely all over the world but not identically. The basics to the dance are three-step with a Cuban hip motion, followed by a tap including a hip movement on the 4th beat.

There are many different styles within bachata which, when danced properly, have a completely different feel and appearance. The most popular styles are Dominican (this involves more upbeat music and footwork) and Sensual (generally danced closer and includes lots of body movements such as body and head rolls).

Zouk

Brazilian Zouk is a modern Brazilian dance which was born in the late 80s from the popular Lambada dance. They adapted the dance to fit other music (mainly Zouk, which was the big music craze at the time, imported from the French Carribean). We also now dance Brazilian Zouk to all different kinds of music, from hiphop and charts to lyrical, classical or anything in between!

Its most obvious distinctive feature is flowing circular upper-body movements for the follower, whipping her hair, and depending on the style can be fast-paced and circular with spins, or slower and more lateral with body isolations and waves.
